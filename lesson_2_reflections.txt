What happens when you initialize a repository? Why do you need to do it?
When you initialize a repository, you have set up your ability to do version-control. You have told the computer that you will be keeping track of files at certain increments, and it is ready for you to start saving/changing.

How is the staging area different from the working directory and the repository?
What value do you think it offers?
The staging area is an interim step from the working directory and the repository. It is a backup, a second chance to make sure what you want to commit is something you actually want to commit.
